USAID Southern Africa

 



Introduction

USAID's VukaNow Activity (“VukaNow”) was a multi-faceted, five-year regional activity, operating in Angola, Botswana, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa, Zambia, and Zimbabwe, with the goal of significantly reducing wildlife crime in southern Africa. The Activity started in March 2018 and ended in March 2023. It complemented five USAID-funded combating wildlife crime (CWC) landscape projects in three Transfrontier Conservation Areas (TFCAs) namely the Great Limpopo (GLTFCA), Kavango-Zambezi (KAZA), and Malawi-Zambia TFCAs, and collaborated with landscape partners and stakeholders, including the Southern Africa Development Community (SADC) Secretariat, governments, and the private sector. VukaNow’s role was to catalyze learning and share information and best practices to enhance collaborative efforts to combat wildlife crime in southern Africa.

 
 

The Challenge

Wildlife crime is a multi-billion-dollar illicit business that is decimating Africa’s iconic wild animal populations, and undermining the economic prosperity and sustainable development of countries and communities throughout southern Africa. It threatens the region’s natural capital and undermines sustainable development from legal nature-based enterprises such as ecotourism. Wildlife crime also threatens social stability and cohesion as it robs citizens of their cultural and natural heritage, while its organized criminal networks threaten regional peace and security.


The African Conservation Trust (ACT) World Youth Wildlife Summit, which was supported by a grant from VukaNow, took place in South Africa on 21-24 September 2019, and was attended by some 200 youth delegates, teachers and community leaders from 16 countries. This event provided a platform for passionate youth, aged 15-18, to engage with conservation experts and experience African wildlife firsthand. It brought together youth leaders from Africa and around the world, empowering them to become local, national, and international ambassadors for wildlife conservation.

 
 

Delegates collaborate at a Malawi-Zambia TFCA law enforcement and antipoaching workshop, December 2019.

VukaNow’s Approach

VukaNow’s role in achieving the goal of significantly reducing wildlife crime in southern Africa was to catalyze learning and sharing of information and best practices and to enhance collaborative efforts to combat wildlife crime in the region. In so doing, VukaNow – together with its partners – worked to coordinate learning across USAID/Southern Africa’s landscape-based CWC activities and to support strategic regional activities that are necessary to address the wildlife crime challenges that fall outside any discrete landscape. This included working with international, regional, national, and local stakeholders to garner an integrated and collaborative approach to combating wildlife crime in the region. VukaNow supported novel and innovative approaches to combating wildlife crime, particularly through the use of purpose-built technology and wildlife forensics. The Activity oversaw a robust grant facility to accelerate specific, targeted interventions to address wildlife crime, including community engagement, and also worked to leverage partnership opportunities with the private sector. In addition, VukaNow worked with journalists and other media actors to facilitate improved transparency and reporting on wildlife crime.

VukaNow recognized that law enforcement alone cannot sufficiently or effectively address wildlife crime. Enforcement actions must be coupled with efforts to incentivize local communities’ participation in and support for combating wildlife crime efforts. VukaNow consolidated and shared lessons with its landscape partners, conservation NGOs, and governments on proven, effective approaches to integrating local communities into combating wildlife crime efforts in the target landscapes.

VukaNow’s partners in implementing the USAID combating wildlife crime portfolio in southern Africa include:

  • World Wildlife Fund US (WWF-US), covering the Kavango-Zambezi (KAZA) Landscape and Northwest Desert Rhino, Namibia 
  • The International Fund for Animal Welfare (IFAW), covering the Malawi-Zambia Transfrontier landscape
  • WWF South Africa, covering the southern (Mozambique-South African) component of the Great Limpopo Transfrontier Conservation Area (GLTFCA)
  • Sustainable Agriculture Technology (SAT), covering the Zimbabwe Lowveld component of the GLTFCA
  • Lowveld Rhino Trust (LRT), covering the Zimbabwe Lowveld component of the GLTFCA
